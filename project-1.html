Analysis of Engagement Metrics for an Online Education Platform

USER EXPERIENCE, 10-09-2023

During my internship at Virtus Universitas, a humanities-focused education company, I tackled a project that aimed at identifying key touchpoints and opportunities for improvement in the online courses from his digital platform. The final goal was to obtain insights to develop strategies that helped build adherence and sustained use.

To do so, our team carried out an investigation that addressed the current public demographic, the consumer journey and the engagement metrics of different courses and formats. The courses offered were about topics like Design Thinking, Digital Transformation and Leadership through great historic examples.

The content was delivered in the form of case studies, videos, articles, storytelling and interactive texts. It was complemented with recommended bibliography in each unit.

Methods

Surveys: Our purpose was to directly gather feedback from the target demographic about their preferences, behaviors, and experiences with the educational content. We distributed surveys via email, focusing on questions related to content engagement, format preferences, and themes of interest.

Engagement Metrics Analysis: Our aim was to quantitatively measure which content formats and themes resonate most with the audience. We utilized web analytics tools to track interactions with the content, such as video view counts, article read times, click-through rates on interactive scenarios, and participation in case studies.

Interviews:In order to gain deeper insights into the consumer journey, such as personal motivations, challenges faced during the learning process, and specific elements that led to increased retention, we arranged one-on-one interviews with a randomly selected sample of the target audience. We set up the questions in order to explote topics related to the educational content and delivery methods.

Content Analysis: Finally, to complement the above strategies, we wanted to qualitatively assess the content itself, determining how well it aligns with the just identified themes and formats that drove engagement. We reviewed the educational material to evaluate the presence of engaging elements, storytelling quality, interactive design, and overall relevance to the target audience's interests and professional needs. After reviewing the contents individually, we would concert a few meetings in a time-span of two weeks to put the ideas in common and agree on the most accurate depiction of the material. All of these methods were brought together in a report that was shared with the relevant company members.

Results

Conclusions

As you can see, we found a high rate for Ethical Leadership and Corporate Responsibility. Videos featuring industry leaders discussing ethical dilemmas and corporate governance have shown to enhance engagement.

Coming second, we found Critical Thinking and Problem-Solving in the format of interactive text-based content. They were mainly case studies that challenged users to apply critical thinking have also led to increased engagement.

In the third position, we found Digital Transformation. Mainly articles and videos that bridge humanities with digital innovation, demonstrating practical applications in business strategy.

Finally, the results were communicated to the manager and a meeting was arranged to decide next steps. What was decided? We recommended to rearrange the content so that the ones related to ethical leadership, critical thinking, and digital transformation were more exposed whithin the digital content structure. It was also better to set up wider visibility in the website for formats like video and text.

There were also suggestions on which next research could be done to shed more light into client behavior. I pointed out that our study highlighted the most appealing content to our main demographic clients, and we did not focus on the interests of other demographics in our data, so we could investigate which content drives the most engagement in these groups in order to attract more of them, diversifying our product.

I next suggested that could also get more insights into the common psychological and sociological characteristics of our main audience, so that, relying on existing literature, predict what new content is most likely to be succesful for them. As Leon G. Schiffman says in "Consumer Insights" (Schiffman, 2019), understanding these factors is crucial because they influence consumer behavior. It will help us analyze the patterns of how our audience interacts with the course content, and identify key drivers of engagement. This involves not just demographic or superficial characteristics, but deeper motivations, values, and preferences that guide their content consumption.
